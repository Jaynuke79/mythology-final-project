Instructions
The final project offers you a chance to be creative. Write a short story or poetry series featuring a character, setting, or myth we’ve covered in class. Create your own visual art adaptation of a myth, god, or place in the medium of your choice. Write and illustrate a graphic novel myth retelling. Make a playlist to create your own jukebox musical of a myth. Plan a film adaptation of a myth, including cast, set, costumes, soundtrack. Feel free to propose other creative and/ or digital options that interest you. For any of these projects, you can base it off a single myth or blend multiple myths/ characters.

Requirements: Written projects should be 4-5 pages long. Visual projects will vary based on medium and size, to be mutually agreed in the proposal. Digital projects should include at least 10 slides, each with images and a min 100 words of text. 

All projects will be accompanied by an artist's statement. This 1-2pg statement should include your inspiration for the project, what you wanted to convey about the myth/ character, and an explanation of the creative choices you made. This statement is 10% of the project grade. 

Submission: The assignment is set up to accept all file types. Please submit written files as .pdf or .doc. Upload media files or submit a link if the file is too large to upload. For physical projects, submit a photo and then bring the project to class. 

Rubric:

A: cohesive myth adaptation, consistent and effective creative elements

B: clear myth connection, multiple creative elements, theme/ purpose unclear 

C: meets length requirements, clear myth connection, light creative elements 

D: below length requirements, predominately summary of myth

F: significantly below length requirements, summary of myth, myth connection unclear
